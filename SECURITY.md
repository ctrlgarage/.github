# Security Policy

We take the security of CtrlGarage's services and code seriously and welcome
coordinated, good-faith vulnerability disclosure.

## Reporting a vulnerability

**Do not open a public issue, pull request, or discussion for a security problem** —
that exposes it to everyone before it can be fixed.

Instead, use either channel:

- **Email** [security@ctrlgarage.dev](mailto:security@ctrlgarage.dev) with details and a
  proof of concept; or
- **GitHub private vulnerability reporting** — on the affected repository, open the
  **Security** tab → **Report a vulnerability**.

Machine-readable contact: <https://ctrlgarage.dev/.well-known/security.txt>

## What to expect

- **Acknowledgement** within **3 business days**.
- A **status update** within **14 days** of acknowledgement.
- We'll keep you informed through triage and remediation, and credit you on request
  once a fix ships.

## Safe harbor

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who act in good faith and follow
this policy — meaning you:

- access only the minimum data needed to demonstrate the issue;
- do not modify, exfiltrate, or destroy data, or degrade our services;
- give us reasonable time to remediate before any public disclosure — **until we
  confirm a fix, or 90 days from report, whichever is sooner**.

## Scope

In scope:

- `ctrlgarage.dev` and its subdomains
- Public repositories under <https://github.com/ctrlgarage>

Out of scope:

- Denial-of-service / volumetric or load testing
- Social engineering of our staff, clients, or vendors
- Physical attacks against our infrastructure or offices
- Findings from automated scanners without a demonstrated, exploitable impact
